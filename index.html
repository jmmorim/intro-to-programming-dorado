About

I am a climate scientist with a multi-disciplinary background bridging coastal, environment, and data science that specializes in physical climate change impacts on offshore and coastal regions. I develop, use and integrate models and statistical methods to assess present and future climate risks and resilience insight from global- to local-scales across governments, businesses, stakeholders and non-profit organizations.

A key part of my work is combining our latest understanding with statistical and mathematical models to provide scientifically credible solutions and robust quantification of uncertainties associated with climate risks to inform adaptation pathways. I also seek to scale up applications of climate data to integrate natural hazard and climate risks into risk management and resilient infrastructure planning for offshore and coastal industry sectors. My key objective is to better understand project risks, limit industry and government disruptions, encourage sustainable climate adaptation and improve interactions between populations and ecosystem services.

Pronouns: he/him
